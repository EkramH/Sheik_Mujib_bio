Sheik Mujibur Rahman

The modern architect of ancient Bengali civilization, one of the most influential political figure of the Indian subcontinent and also the first president of independent Bangladesh, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was given the title 'Bangabandhu' for his boundless love and contribution for the homeland and for the countrymen.

Early Life

Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was born on March 17, 1920, in Tungipara village of Patggati UP under the then Gopalganj subdivision of Faridpur district. His father, Sheikh Lutfar Rahman, was a serestadar (court clerk) of the Gopalganj Sessions Court and his mother's name is Sayera Khatun. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was the third child of a family of four daughters and two sons. His sisters are Fatima Begum, Achia Begum, Helen and Lyle; His younger brother's name is Sheikh Abu Naser. In 1927, Sheikh Mujib began his studies at Gimadanga Primary School. He was admitted to Gopalganj Public School in 1929. In 1937, he was admitted to the seventh grade at Mathuranath Institute Mission School in Gopalganj. He could not continue his schooling for four years from 1934. Because he had to undergo surgery because of a complex disease in his eye and it took a long time to recover. He passed matriculation from Gopalganj Missionary School. Political Activism: The political life of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was started at the missionary school in 1939. During this year, Sher-e-Bangla AK Fazlul Haque, the Chief Minister of the then Bengal Presidency and later Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy, the Prime Minister of the then Bengal had visited the school. He approached them with a group based on the demands of the school's roof renovation. He joined the All India Muslim Students Federation in 1940. After passing the Entrance in 1942, he admitted to Calcutta Islamia College for studying law. In 1943, he joined the Bengal Muslim League and came close to the Bengali Muslim leader Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was elected councillor of the Bengal Muslim League in 1943. Sheikh Mujib played a leading role in the conference of the Nikhil Bengal Muslim Students League in Kushtia. He was nominated secretary of the Kolkata based Faridpur District Association. Two years later, he was elected secretary-general of the Islamia College Student Union. In 1947, He received his BA degree from Islamia College. He joined Suhrawardy in various political activities to control the riot during the India-Pakistan division. After the separation of Pakistan-India, Sheikh Mujib returned to East Pakistan and admitted to the Law Department of Dhaka University. He established The East Pakistan Muslim Chhatra League on 4th January 1948. By which he became one of the leading student leaders of the then East Pakistan.

Early Politics & Language movement

On February 23, 1948, Khawaja Nazimuddin, the then Prime Minister said that Urdu will be the only state language of Pakistan. His comments triggered adverse reactions throughout East Pakistan. Sheikh Mujib immediately decided to launch a protest against the Muslim League's decision. On March 2, 1948, a conference was held with the leaders of various political parties at Fazlul Haque Hall of Dhaka University. At the conference, the policy of the movement against the Muslim League was discussed while Sheikh Mujib offered a proposal. From there the decision to form an all-party movement council ‘Sarba Dalio Sangram Parishad’ was made. And following the call of the council, a strike was observed in Dhaka on March 11 while some political activists, including Sheikh Mujib, were arrested during the strike. On March 15, Sheikh Mujib and other student leaders were released in the face of intense student protests. Sheikh Mujib declared a nationwide student strike on March 17 to protest the then East Pakistan police’s brutal activities. He then organized a movement to secure the rights of the fourth-grade employees of Dhaka University on March 19. He was arrested again and expelled from the university on September 11 following the issue.

Establishment of Awami League

Sheikh Mujib was released from jail on 21st January 1949. Sheikh Mujib left the Muslim League and joined to the new party after Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy and Maulana Bhasani formed the East Pakistan Awami Muslim League on June 23, 1949. He was elected joint general secretary of the party's East Pakistan section. The Awami Muslim League staged an anti-famine procession in Dhaka on the occasion of the then Prime Minister Liaquat Ali Khan's arrival in East Pakistan early January 1950. Sheikh Mujib was arrested for leading the procession and he was sentenced to jailed for two years. On January 26, 1952, Khawaja Nazimuddin announced that Urdu would be Pakistan's only official language. After the announcement, he played a role in giving instructions from the jail. He was released from jail on February 26.

Juktofront Election

On 14th November 1953, a decision was made to form a Juktofront with other parties to participate in the general election. In the general elections on March 10, 1954, the Juktofront won 223 of the 237 seats, of which the Awami Muslim League won 143 seats alone. Sheikh Mujib was passed from the Gopalganj constituency. He was given the responsibility of the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry on May 15. On May 29, the central government disbanded the Juktofront. After returning from Karachi on May 30, he was detained at the airport. He was released on December 23 of that year. On 5th June 1955, Sheikh Mujib was nominated a member of the Legislative Council. On 17 June, the Awami Muslim League presented a 21-point demand in a rally at Paltan Maidan, which included the autonomy of East Pakistan. The term 'Muslim' was dropped from the party's name in a special session of Awami Muslim League on October 21-23 in 1955. Sheikh Mujib was re-elected secretary-general of the party. In the Awami League meeting with the chief minister on February 3, the demand to include autonomy in the draft constitution was proposed from the party. On July 14, a proposal was made against the military presence in the state administration, which was submitted to the government by Sheikh Mujib. On 16th September, Sheikh Mujib joined the Coalition Government and simultaneously got the responsibility of the Ministry of Industry, Commerce, Labor, Anti-Corruption and Rural Assistance. He resigned from the cabinet on May 30, 1957, to spend full time for the party. On August 7, he made an official visit to China and the Soviet Union. On October 7, the President of Pakistan Major General Iskandar Mirza and the army chief Ayub Khan declared that all political activities were banned by imposing military rule in the country. On 11th October of that year, Bangabandhu was again arrested. He was released from jail in 1961 and started working to achieve independence of Bangladesh. He was again arrested on February 6, 1962, under the Public Safety Act. He was released on the same month after the removal of a four-year martial rule on June 2. On June 25, he, along with other political leaders, started fight against various political issues imposed by Ayub Khan. On September 24, he went to Lahore and formed a National Democratic Front along with Shahid Suhrawardy. Throughout the month of October, he visited various places of Bengal to get support for the Juktofront along with Shahid Suhrawardy.

The election in 1970

During the trial, on January 5, 1969, the Central Student Movement Parishad submitted their 11-point demand, which included six points of Sheikh Mujib. Following the decision of this council, mass agitation was begun demanding the withdrawal of the Agartala conspiracy case. This movement is known as the 69’s mass uprising. After the agitation, the case was withdrawn and all the accused including Sheikh Mujib were released. Sheikh Mujib was given the title of 'Bangabandhu' at a meeting of the central student movement council at the then Racecourse ground (Suhrawardy Udyan) on February 23 that year. At an all-party conference at the call of Ayub Khan in 1969, Sheikh Mujib called to recognize the demands of other political parties, including the six-point, and but was rejected and then he walked out the summit. In a meeting on the death anniversary of Suhrawardy on December 5, Sheikh Mujib declared that from now on, East Pakistan would be called 'Bangladesh'. Subsequently, Mujib was able to build huge public mandate in East Pakistan and by the year 1970 he had emerged as one of the most influential leaders of the Indian subcontinent. In the election in 1971, the Awami League, led by Sheikh Mujib, won an absolute majority in the provincial legislature. The result of the elections creates polarization between the two parts of Pakistan. Zulfiqar Ali Bhutto, the leader of the largest political party in West Pakistan, opposes Mujib's autonomy. He announced that he would not accept the government if President Yahya Khan called on Sheikh Mujib to form a government. Most West Pakistani army officers and Islamic political parties oppose Sheikh Mujib's incoming Prime Minister.

Establishment of Bangladesh

In the midst of political instability, Yahya was late to call Parliament. Bengalis understand that the Awami League, the party of Mujib, will not be allowed to form a government even though it has the majority. Sheikh Mujib called for independence at a rally at the Racecourse ground on March 7 and prepared the people for a total non-cooperation movement. Meanwhile, Yahya Khan issued martial law. Declaring the Awami League banned, he ordered to arrest Sheikh Mujib and other Awami League leaders. The Pakistani military launched Operation Searchlight on March 25 to suppress political and public dissatisfaction. When the operation was started, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman announced the independence of Bangladesh over wireless from his residence of Dhanmondi 32 on March 26, 1971. On that day, he was arrested and taken to West Pakistan and kept in a tight security in a Faisalabad jail. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was the President of Bangladesh Government and the Chief of the Armed Forces, established in Mujibnagar on April 17, 1971. In his absence, Deputy President Syed Nazrul Islam served as the temporary head of state. The Pakistan Army's operation to control the situation soon turned into violence and bloodshed. With the help of razakars, the Pakistani army attacked ordinary people including Bengali intellectuals, politicians and union leaders. East Bengal members working in the Pakistan army and police regiments declared a revolt and Awami League members formed the government of Bangladesh under the leadership of Tajuddin Ahmed in Kolkata. After nine months of the war, the Pakistani army surrendered on 16 December and the Awami League leaders formed the government back in Dhaka. The Pakistani government released Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib on January 8, 1972. He then met Indian Prime Minister Indira Gandhi in New Delhi via London. He returned to Bangladesh on January 10 1972.

Assassination

On August 15, a group of stray army officers were engaged in a brutal massacre at the Dhanmondi residence by the tank. Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, his family members and his personal employees were killed at that time. Only Sheikh Hasina and Sheikh Rehana were survived as they were staying in West Germany. The body of Bangabandhu was taken to his birthplace Tungipara by helicopter and buried under military supervision. The other dead were buried at Banani graveyard in Dhaka.

Personal Life

Sheikh Mujib, 14, was married to 3-year-old Begum Fazilatunnesa. After 9 years of marriage, Sheikh Mujib and Fazilutunnesa began their conjugal life in 1942. The couple was bestowed with two daughters and three sons. The daughters are Sheikh Hasina and Sheikh Rehana. The sons are Sheikh Kamal, Sheikh Jamal and Sheikh Russell. The anti-liberation power wanted to destroy the spirit of the War of Liberation by killing the father of the Nation Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. Although they conspired to erase Bangabandhu's name from history, the Bengali nation resisted it and brought the Awami League to power. The Awami League, led by his daughter Sheikh Hasina, formed the government three times in a row from 2009. Bangladesh is now on the highway of development, inspired by Mujib ideals. On the occasion of the birth centenary of this great leader, 'Mujibborsho' will be observed all over the world from March 17
